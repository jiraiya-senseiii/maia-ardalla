About

A short profile & standing remarks

Maia Ojerinde Ardalla is a writer, researcher and creative practitioner whose work moves between architecture, poetry, design, museology and film.

At the British Film Institute she leads outreach as Outreach Manager, specialising in underserved audiences, creative programming and the long, patient labour of cultural participation. Her practice is shaped by co-design — working alongside emerging artists, communities and young people, rather than for them.

She has written for the V&A Blog (Show + Tell + Share) and sat as an expert panellist for New London Architecture, contributing to East Bank: A New Cultural Identity in September 2022.

As a spoken word artist, she has performed at The Night Gallery in Brixton Village for the London Festival of Architecture — a programme that took In Common as its theme, and her work as one of its clearest expressions.

Creative practice as a mechanism and catalyst for socio-political transformation. — a working principle

In Common

A spoken word performance for an architecture festival that asked what we share, who we share it with, and what a city can mean when built from the ground up — by, with, and for its people.

On practice

Poetry, for Maia, is not separate from the rest of the work — it is the same instrument turned a different way. The same belief in gathering, the same listening, the same insistence on cultural participation as a form of repair.
